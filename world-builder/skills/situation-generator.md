# Situation Generator

Generate a complete, playable situation that creates immediate tension and urgency.

## Input

Provide context that may include:
- Genre or tone
- Time period or setting
- Characters involved
- Inciting event or conflict
- Constraints or themes

If elements are missing, make strong choices that create tension.

## Critical Rules

- DO NOT write dialogue
- DO NOT analyze psychology or inner thoughts
- ONLY describe external reality, pressures, and circumstances
- Write in neutral, objective language
- Be concrete and specific, never vague
- Make the situation immediately playable

## Output Format

Generate exactly these seven sections:

### 1. Location & Time
- Where the scene takes place
- Time of day/year/era
- Environmental factors (noise, weather, confinement, public vs private)
- Specific physical space details

### 2. Immediate Circumstances
- What is happening RIGHT NOW as the scene begins
- What has just occurred that makes this moment unavoidable
- Why the character can't delay or escape
- The triggering event

### 3. Power Dynamics
- Who holds power and why
- Is power shifting, unstable, or contested?
- What leverage, status, or authority is present
- Specific source of power

### 4. Relationships Present
- Who else is involved or nearby
- Character's history with them
- Unresolved tensions, secrets, or obligations
- Past events that matter now

### 5. Stakes & Consequences
- What the character gains if things go well
- What they risk losing if things go badly
- Tangible, immediate, specific consequences
- No abstract stakes

### 6. Constraints
- Physical limits (space, injury, surveillance)
- Social/moral limits (reputation, loyalty, law, fear)
- Time pressure or deadlines
- Why the character cannot simply leave or refuse

### 7. Emotional Pressure Points
- External triggers that could provoke strong emotion
- Situational reminders of past wounds or desires
- Environmental elements that escalate tension
- Observable triggers, not internal states

## Example Output

**1. Location & Time**: A cramped interrogation room in the precinct basement, 2 AM. Fluorescent lights hum. No windows. One door, locked from outside. Coffee stains on the metal table.

**2. Immediate Circumstances**: Detective Morrison just slid a photograph across the table—your business partner's body in the warehouse. "We found your fingerprints on the murder weapon." The recording light is on.

**3. Power Dynamics**: Morrison controls the room, the door, and the evidence. But she needs a confession—the physical evidence alone won't hold. You know something she doesn't: there's a witness who saw the real killer.

**4. Relationships Present**: Morrison arrested your brother three years ago. He's still in prison. She knows you blame her. Her partner watches through the one-way glass.

**5. Stakes & Consequences**: Confess and face 25 to life. Stay silent and they hold you for 48 hours—long enough for the real killer to disappear, or to find you. Talk your way out and you walk, but Morrison will be watching.

**6. Constraints**: No lawyer present yet—you waived your right in the car, thinking this was routine. Your phone is in evidence. No one knows you're here. The door only opens from outside.

**7. Emotional Pressure Points**: The photo shows your partner's face—eyes open, surprised. The same expression he had when you told him about the deal. Morrison keeps calling him "the victim" instead of using his name.
