# NPC Roleplay

Generate authentic in-character responses for NPCs during gameplay.

## Input Required

- **Character Info**: NPC's personality, motivation, relationships, speech patterns
- **Current Situation**: What's happening in the scene
- **Prompt**: What the NPC needs to respond to (PC dialogue, action, or event)

## Quick Prep (Internal)

Before responding, consider:
1. What does this character want RIGHT NOW?
2. What are they afraid of in this moment?
3. What tactics would they use? (charm, threaten, deflect, probe, dismiss)
4. What's the subtext—what do they mean vs. what they say?
5. How does their body language and voice reflect their state?

## Response Guidelines

### Stay In Character
- Use the character's established speech patterns and vocabulary
- React based on their personality and motivations
- Don't break character to explain choices

### Show, Don't Tell
- Reveal character through action and dialogue
- Use subtext—what's unsaid matters
- Include brief physical beats: [pauses], [leans forward], [voice drops]

### Match the Tone
- **Dark**: Guarded, desperate, morally gray responses
- **Heroic**: Bold, colorful, theatrical
- **Comedy**: Quirky, absurd, memorable for wrong reasons
- **Mystery**: Evasive, calculated, everyone has secrets
- **Horror**: Wrong, unsettling, too calm or too intense

### Keep It Playable
- 2-4 sentences of dialogue/action is usually enough
- Leave room for PC response
- Create hooks for continued interaction
- Don't monologue unless dramatically appropriate

## Output Format

Provide the NPC's response in character:

**[NPC Name]**: [Their dialogue and actions]

Include bracketed beats for physical actions: [crosses arms], [glances at the door], [smile doesn't reach eyes]

## Example

**Input**: Moe the bartender, suspicious and protective of his regulars. Homer just asked if he's seen anyone unusual tonight.

**Output**:

**Moe**: [polishes the same glass he's been holding for five minutes] "Unusual?" [snorts] "Homer, look around. Everyone in here is unusual. That's why they drink here instead of somewhere nice." [leans closer, voice dropping] "But if you're asking about the guy in the corner who's been nursing the same beer for three hours and watching the door... I ain't seen nothing. And neither have you. Capisce?"
