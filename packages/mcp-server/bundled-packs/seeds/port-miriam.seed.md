# Port Miriam, City of Wet Ashes

A 1947 noir city in perpetual rain. Port Miriam is built on graft, harbor smoke, and institutional rot. Every official is on someone's payroll; every honest cop has been pushed off the force; every reporter's biggest story has been killed by their own editor. The Aldermen run a Syndicate that owns the docks, the Cannery District, and three precincts of the police. The protagonists are people who still remember how to be ashamed — investigators, fixers, journalists, getaway drivers, all walking the line between complicity and conscience.

Themes: corruption as infrastructure, identity and performance, the cost of knowing, class and complicity. Loyalty is the only currency that hasn't been counterfeited yet.

Magic: rare. Lethality: high. Tech: industrial-1940s. Tone: cynical but not hopeless — moral ambiguity is the weather.
