# Treehouse Anthology — A Suburban Halloween

A Halloween anthology in a yellow-tinged suburban town where the cul-de-sacs are too clean and the trick-or-treaters are too small. Beneath the manicured lawns is a literal Hell — and tonight it has reached up through the storm drains and taken the youngest member of one cartoon family. The rest must descend, in costume, with whatever's still in the candy bowl, to bring her back. The underworld is run by accountants of debt; the demons want what every demon wants, which is a deal; and the family will absolutely make every wrong deal before they figure out the right one.

Themes: surreal-comedic dread, suburban gothic, family loyalty as the only real magic, cartoon physics meeting folk horror, holiday-special tonal whiplash, what families owe each other and what they refuse to.

Magic: pervasive but absurd. Lethality: medium — cartoon-soft when it's funny, real when it's not. Tech: 1990s American suburbia. Tone: Treehouse-of-Horror anthology — surreal, comedic, eerie, with heart. Original characters and town; no trademarked names.
