# The Ashen Sovereignty of Aethelgard

A dark high-fantasy kingdom in its final dying decades. The Ashen Sovereignty of Aethelgard once spanned three continents; now it is a single sere realm defended by the last surviving High Elf, a ranger-king whose memory of better ages is the only thing holding the dragon's eternal hunger at bay. Faded magical orders, bone-deep weariness, the quiet beauty of things being lost on purpose. The dragon is patient; the elves are not — they have always known that they were dying. The protagonists are the kingdom's last hope: rangers, lorekeepers, dragon-cultists who think the dragon is salvation, oathsworn warriors who know it isn't.

Themes: graceful decline, memory as power, last-stands without heroism, the dignity of doomed loyalty, the politics of a dying court that still pretends it isn't.

Magic: present but waning. Lethality: high. Tech: high-medieval. Tone: elegiac, reverent of what was, defiant of what comes.
