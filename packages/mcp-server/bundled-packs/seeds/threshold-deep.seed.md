# Threshold Deep

A dying deep-space station at the edge of charted territory. Meridian Anchorage was a refueling outpost on the route to nowhere; now it has been alone for so long that nobody onboard remembers what the route was supposed to lead to. Then the signals start arriving — old signals, in a language nobody onboard remembers learning. The crew of forty-three is the last set of people who could hear the signal and recognize the danger. What they don't know yet is that something is already aboard, has been for years, and has been listening to them too.

Themes: the wrongness of empty deep space, communication across deep time, body horror as message, the loneliness of being noticed by something patient.

Magic: none. Tech: futuristic. Lethality: high. Tone: claustrophobic, slow-burning, sci-fi cosmic horror, body-horror-adjacent. _Solaris_ meets _Event Horizon_ meets _Annihilation_ set on a derelict station that hasn't been derelict long enough.
